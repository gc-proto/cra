Our review of your application

Once we have received your application, we will review it and inform you of our decision in writing. We may contact you if we need more information.

If we accept your application, you will still have to pay the tax but you will receive relief from prosecution. You may also receive penalty and/or partial interest relief.

VDP category programs

Depending on the situation, applications are placed into 1 of 3 programs that offer different types of relief.

General program

Intended for taxpayers who want to come forward and correct unintentional errors.

If your application is accepted into the General program

We will provide the following relief:

  • you will not be charged penalties
  • you will not be referred for criminal prosecution related to the information you disclose
  • you may be eligible for a reduced interest rate on the amount you owe
Limited program

Applies to taxpayers who intentionally avoided their tax obligations

If your application is accepted into the Limited program

The following will happen:

  • you will not be referred for criminal prosecution related to the information you disclose
  • you will not be charged for gross negligence penalties; however, you will be charged other penalties as applicable
  • no interest relief is provided
Wash transactions program

Generally one where a supplier has failed to charge and collect GST/HST from a registrant who is entitled to a full input tax credit.

If accepted into the Wash transactions program

The following will happen:

  • you will not be referred for criminal prosecution related to the information disclosed
  • you may be eligible for full penalty and interest relief on the transactions disclosed

Factors considered for determining the appropriate program of relief

In all programs, the following factors are considered:

  • the dollar amounts involved
  • the number of years of non-compliance
  • the sophistication of the taxpayer or registrant
  • how quickly the taxpayer or registrant acted to correct their non-compliance upon its discovery
  • if the disclosure is made after a CRA statement about its intended specific focus of compliance (for example, the launch of a compliance project or campaign) or following broad-based CRA correspondence (for example, a letter issued to taxpayers working in a particular sector about a compliance issue).

Our decision about your application

Once we have your application, we will check to make sure it meets the necessary conditions of a valid application.

If after our initial review, we determine that your application is missing information and/or documentation, we may deny your application. At times, we may contact you to request the missing information/ documentation and negotiate a reply date.

If your application is denied as not complete, because the information was not submitted by the due date, the CRA will not consider a request for a second administrative review and we will deny any later application for the same matter.

If your application is approved

If your application is accepted, you will receive a letter with the following information:

  • The track, General, Limited, or Wash Transactions, that your application was accepted under
  • The taxation year(s) eligible for relief

If your application is denied

If your application is denied, you will receive a letter giving you the reason for the denial.

Denied due to not complete

If your application was denied as not complete, due to information not being sent by a provided due date, the CRA will not consider a request for a second administrative review and will deny any later application for the same matter.

For details: paragraphs 59-60 of Information Circular IC00-1R6 or paragraphs 66-68 of the GST/HST Memorandum 16-5

Disagreeing with our decision

If you disagree with the CRA's decision, you still have options.

Asking for a second administrative review

If you believe the Minister of National Revenue has not been fair or reasonable, you can request a second administrative review or apply to the Federal Court (of Canada) for a review of the Minister’s decision (Judicial Review). Generally, taxpayers should request a second administrative review from the CRA before applying for a Judicial Review with the Federal Court.

To request a second review, send your written request to the Assistant Director of the Shawinigan National Verification and Collections Centre.

Asking for a judicial review

To request a judicial review, the taxpayer must send a completed Form 301, Notice of Application, with the appropriate filing fee, to the registrar of the Federal Court. For more information, see paragraphs 61-64 of Information Circular IC00-1R6 or paragraphs 69-72 of GST/HST Memorandum 16-5.
