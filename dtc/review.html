Our review of your application

After the CRA receives your application, we assess it to determine if you are eligible for the DTC. We base our analysis on the information given by the medical practitioner.

If we need more information, we may contact you or the medical practitioner.

How long it will take

The CRA aims to process your application and send you a notice of determination within 8 weeks of receiving it. It may take longer if information is missing.

Check CRA processing times

If you send your application with your tax return, the CRA will review your application before assessing your tax return.

If we need more information

In some cases, the CRA may send the medical practitioner a letter asking for more information, such as documents to support your application. The letter will contain a reference number and instructions on what to do.

Our decision about your application

The CRA will send you a notice of determination to inform you of our decision.

If your application is approved

If your notice of determination says your application is approved, you may claim the tax credit on your income tax return. Or you may transfer the credit to a parent or guardian, or a spouse or common law partner.

Your notice of determination will show which year(s) you can claim the DTC. You don't need to re-apply for the credit every year, unless we tell you to.

The DTC also opens doors to other federal, provincial, and territorial programs. Your notice of determination may include information about other programs that are dependent on DTC eligibility, such as:

  • Registered disability savings plan
  • Canada workers benefit
  • Child disability benefit

Some approved applications expire after a certain number of years and you will have to re-apply. When your eligibility is about to expire, we will notify you 1 year in advance, as well as in the year it expires.

You can view your DTC information in My Account.

You must inform the CRA in writing if your medical condition improves to the point that you no longer meet the criteria for the DTC.

If your application is denied

If your DTC application is denied, your notice of determination will explain why.

Check your copy of your application, Form T2201, Disability Tax Credit Certificate, against the reason(s) given. We base our decision on the information given by the medical practitioner.

Disagreeing with our decision

If you disagree with the CRA's decision, you still have options:

Call to discuss: Option 1 of 4

You may call us to ask questions or to discuss your application.

Contact the CRA

Request a review or submit new documentation

You may request a review of your application or submit relevant medical information you have not already sent, such as:

  • new or updated medical reports
  • a letter from a medical practitioner who is familiar with your situation

The information you provide should describe how the impairment(s) affects you.

There are 2 ways to do this:

Online

Sign into CRA My Account and select "Submit documents" in the top right corner.

File an objection

You may challenge the CRA's decision by filing an objection within 90 days of getting your notice of determination.
