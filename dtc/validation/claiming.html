Who can claim the DTC

The individual with the impairment may claim the DTC on their income tax return once the CRA has approved the application. This may reduce the amount of income tax they have to pay.

The DTC is a non-refundable tax credit. If the credit does not reduce your balance owing for the current year, it is not refunded.

Transferring the credit

If you do not claim the entire disability amount on your tax return, you may transfer some or all of it to a supporting family member.

Who qualifies as a supporting family member

A supporting family member is someone who the individual with the impairment depends on for at least 1 of the necessities of life (food, shelter, and clothing).

The family member must be one of the following:

  • a spouse or common law partner
  • a child or grandchild
  • a spouse or common-law partner's child or grandchild
  • a parent, grandparent, brother, sister, uncle, aunt, niece, or nephew
  • a spouse or common-law partner's parent, grandparent, brother, sister, uncle, aunt, niece, or nephew

The supporting family member's information, including their SIN and details of the type of support they provide, should have been entered on the DTC application form (Part A – 2).

Changing who claims the transferred credit

The CRA requires written confirmation to transfer the credit to a supporting family member whose SIN is not on the DTC application form (Part A - 2).

If we need more information

In some cases, the CRA may send you a letter asking for additional information to confirm who is claiming the credit. The letter will contain a reference number and instructions on what to do.

Claiming for 2020

Approved applicants who are 18 years and older (on the last day of the year) may claim the base disability amount. Those who are 17 years and younger (on the last day of the year) may also claim the additional youth supplement.

Disability amounts for the 2020 tax year:

18 and older: Option 1 of 2

  • $8,576 (base amount)
  • equals 8,576

17 and younger : Option 2 of 2

  • $8,576 (base amount)
  • plus $5,003 (additional youth supplement)
  • equals 13,579

How to claim the DTC on your tax return

To claim the credit for the current year, you must enter the disability amount on your tax return. Any unused amount may be transferred to a supporting family member.

Claim for yourself

If you are the individual with the impairment, enter the disability amount on:
Line 31600 - Disability amount for self

Claim for a dependant

If you are a parent or guardian of the individual with the impairment, enter the disability amount on:
Line 31800 - Disability amount transferred from a dependant

Claim for a spouse

If you are the spouse or common law partner of the person with the impairment, enter the disability amount on:
Line 32600 - Amounts transferred from your spouse or common-law partner

Medical practitioner fees

If the medical practitioner charged a fee for completing the DTC application or for providing information, you may be able to claim it as medical expense on lines 33099 and 33199 of your tax return.

Tax return processing times

Processing times vary according to how you file. Electronic filing is generally faster than paper filing.

Check CRA processing times

If you submit your DTC application with your tax return, we will review your application before assessing your tax return.

Claiming for past years

If you were eligible for the DTC in past years but did not claim it, you can ask the CRA to apply the credit to previous tax returns going back up to 10 years.

If you did not check the box on the application form asking the CRA to adjust your previous returns, you may either:

  • ask the CRA to adjust your previous return(s)
  • adjust your previous return(s) yourself using change my return
