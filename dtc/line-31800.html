Claiming the Disability tax credit

If your dependant is eligible for the Disability tax credit (DTC) and does not claim the amount on their tax return (line 31600), they may transfer it to you. This would allow you to claim all or part of the disability amount on your tax return.

A dependant may be your or your spouse's or common-law partner's parent, grandparent, child, grandchild, brother, sister, aunt, uncle, niece, or nephew.

A dependant may not be your spouse of common law partner.

Attach a note to your tax return with your dependant's name, social insurance number, and relationship to you if you are not attaching the DTC application Form T2201, Disability Tax Credit Certificate with your tax return.

Who can claim for a dependant

You may be able to claim all or part of your dependant's disability amount if you meet the following:

  • Your dependant was resident in Canada at any time in 2020
  • Your dependant was dependent on you for all or some of the basic necessities of life (food, shelter, or clothing)
  • One of the following applies:

    You claimed an amount on line 30400 for that dependant, or you could have if you did not have a spouse or common-law partner and if the dependant did not have any income

    You claimed an amount on line line 30450 for that dependant, or you could have if they had no income and had been 18 years or older in 2020

How to claim for a dependant

If your dependant was under 18 years

To claim for a dependant who was under 18 years at the end of the year:

  1. Complete the chart for line 31600 on the Worksheet for the return in the Income Tax Package to calculate the supplement that your dependant may be able to claim.
  2. Complete the chart for line 31800 on the Worksheet for the return in the Income tax package to calculate your claim for each dependant.
  1. Calculate the supplement that your dependant may be able to claim
    Complete the chart for line 31600 on the Worksheet for the return in the Income Tax Package .
  2. Calculate your claim for each dependant
    Complete the chart for line 31800 on the Worksheet for the return in the Income tax package and enter the amount on line 31800 of your return.

If you pay child support

You cannot claim the disability amount transferred from a dependant for a child for whom you had to pay child support.

However, special rules may apply if you were separated from your spouse or common-law partner for only part of 2020 due to a breakdown in your relationship.

Exceptions to claiming

You cannot claim the unused part of the disability amount if the spouse or common-law partner of your dependant is:

  • claiming the disability amount for them already
  • claiming any other non-refundable tax credit (other than medical expenses) for them

If you or anyone else paid for attendant care, or for care in an establishment, special rules may apply.

For details: Attendant care or care in an establishment

Splitting the amount with another supporting person

To split the unused part of the disability amount with another supporting person, attach a note to your tax return with the name and social insurance number of the other person who is claiming the amount.

The total claimed for the dependant cannot be more than the maximum amount allowed for that dependant.
