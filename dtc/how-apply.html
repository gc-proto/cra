Get ready to apply

Applying for the DTC involves the individual who has the impairment and a medical practitioner who can certify the effects of the impairment.

Find a medical practitioner who can certify your impairment

The following medical practitioners can certify impairments:

Medical doctor

All impairments

Nurse practitioner

All impairments

Optometrist

Occupational therapist

Walking, feeding, dressing

Physiotherapist

Walking

Psychologist

Mental functions

Speech-language pathologist

Speaking


Possible medical practitioner fees

If your medical practitioner charges a fee for completing the DTC application, you are responsible to pay it. However, you may be able to claim it as a medical expense on your tax return.

Determine who may claim the credit

If your application is approved, you may claim the credit on your tax return. If there is any remaining amount, you may transfer it to a supporting family member.

Who is a supporting family member

A supporting family member is someone who the individual with the impairment depends on for at least 1 of the necessities of life (food, shelter, and clothing).

They may be either a:

  • spouse
  • spouse's parent, grandparent, brother, sister, uncle, aunt, niece, or nephew who resides in Canada at any time in the year
  • spouse's child or grandchild
  • common-law partner
  • common-law partner's parent, grandparent, brother, sister, uncle, aunt, niece, or nephew who resides in Canada at any time in the year
  • common-law partner's child or grandchild

The supporting family member will need to provide some information about themselves, including their SIN and details of the type of support they provide, on the application form (Part A - 2).

For details: Transferring the credit

Consider when to apply

You may apply for the DTC at any time during the year.

However, if you submit your application at the same time that you file your tax return, there may be a delay in your tax assessment. We will review your DTC application before we assess your tax return.

To avoid a possible delay, submit your DTC application before you file your tax return.

Complete the application

There are 2 options to complete the DTC application:

  • Apply with the digital form
  • Apply with the paper form

Digital form

  1. Medical practitioner fills out the "Medical practitioner's section" (Part B)

    The medical practitioner opens the DTC Digital application for medical practitioners and completes Part B by answering questions about the effects of the impairment.

    Types of questions
    • Basic information about the medical practitioner
    • Patient information
    • Information about the impairment(s)
    • If your patient might be eligible under the "Cumulative effect of significant limitations" category, you will have a few additional eligibility questions to answer at the end
    How medical practitioners assess patients

    Medical practitioners use their best professional judgement when filling out Part B.

    They may base their assessment on:

    1. the symptoms reported by the patient
    2. their knowledge of the patient's medical history
    3. their knowledge of the effects of the impairment(s) as they relate to the patient's medical condition

    The CRA bases its decision about eligibility on the information that medical practitioners provide in Part B.

    After filling in Part B, the medical practitioner must:

    • print and sign the form to certify it
    • give the printed certified form to their patient to fill out Part A
    Tip: To keep a copy for your records, save or print the PDF.

  2. Applicant completes the "Individual's section" (Part A)

    The individual with the impairment or their legal representative fills out Part A of the printed form.

    Tip: If you want the CRA to adjust your tax returns, indicate this in Part A - 3.
  3. Send it to us

    Either the applicant or the medical practitioner may submit the form to the CRA.

Paper form

  1. Applicant fills out the "Individual's section" (Part A)

    The individual with the impairment or their legal representative fills out Part A of the application, Form T2201, Certificate for the Disability Tax Credit.

    Tip: If you want the CRA to adjust your tax returns, indicate this in Part A - 3.

    After filling out Part A, the applicant gives the printed form to their medical practitioner to complete and sign Part B.

  2. Medical practitioner completes the "Medical practitioner's" section (Part B)

    The medical practitioner answers a few questions and fills out the impairment section(s) that apply to their patient.

    Types of questions
    • Basic information about the medical practitioner
    • Patient information
    • Information about the impairment(s)
    • If the section called "Cumulative effect of significant limitations" applies, you must also fill in the individual sections for the impairments that contribute to the cumulative effect
    How medical practitioners assess patients

    Medical practitioners use their best professional judgement when filling out Part B.

    They may base their assessment on:

    • the symptoms reported by the patient
    • their knowledge of the patient's medical history
    • their knowledge of the effects of the impairment(s) as they relate to the patient's medical condition

    The CRA bases its decision about eligibility on the information that medical practitioners provide in Part B.

    After filling in Part B, the medical practitioner must complete and sign the "Certification" section on the last page.

  3. Send it to us

    Either the medical practitioner or the applicant may submit the form to the CRA.
