How to apply

It is important that you provide the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) with a complete and accurate description of the circumstances to explain why your situation merits relief. To support a request, you should provide all relevant information including the following, where applicable:

  • your social insurance number (SIN), account number, partnership number, trust account number, business number (BN), or any other identification number assigned to you by the CRA
  • the tax year(s) or fiscal period(s) involved
  • any documentation relevant to your qualifying situation:
Financial hardship

You will also need to provide:

  • A completed Form RC376, Taxpayer Relief Request – Statement of Income and Expenses and Assets and Liabilities for Individuals
  • Documents to support your financial disclosure, such as:
  • current mortgage statements
  • property assessments
  • rental agreements
  • loans and monthly bills
  • bank and credit card for the most recent 3 months
  • current investment statements
  • loans and monthly bills
  • Business clients should include a copy of their income and expense statement and assets and liabilities statement.
Death of a loved one

You will also need to provide:

  • A death certificate or obituary
An accident, serious illness or emotional distress

You will also need to provide:

  • A doctor's certificate or letter indicating the type of illness, length of treatment (onset of illness and the date or expected date of recovery)
  • Hospital dates, if they apply
  • An explanations regarding the effect the medical condition may have had on meeting your tax obligations
Natural or human-made disaster

You will also need to provide:

  • Insurance statements
  • Fire, police or another report in evidence of the disaster
Civil disturbance

You will also need to provide:

  • As such events are public knowledge, you will likely only need a description of the disturbance
CRA mistake or delay

You will also need to provide:

  • An explanation in writing of the details and timelines of the delay or error caused by the CRA

Submit your request

Online (personal)

For tax relief related to an individual, you can sign into My Account or Represent a client and use submit a request through the:

  • Request relief of penalties and interest service
Sign in to My Account Register

Alternative: Represent a client

Online (business)

Download and complete Form RC4288

  • Form RC4288, Request for Taxpayer Relief - Cancel or Waive Penalties or Interest

Then submit the form online in My Business Account using the:

  • Submit documents service
Sign in to My Business Account Register
By mail (personal or business)

Download and complete Form RC4288

  • Form RC4288, Request for Taxpayer Relief - Cancel or Waive Penalties or Interest

Then send the form to the tax centre or services office for your region:
