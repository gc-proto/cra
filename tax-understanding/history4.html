Timeline of events

  • 1933: The federal government added a non-resident withholding tax.
  • 1935: The federal government created a gift tax.
  • 1939: In 1930, only three provinces (Prince Edward Island, Manitoba, and British Columbia) taxed personal income. However, by 1939, seven provinces were collecting personal income tax.
  • 1948: The Income Tax Act was passed.
  • 1952: Under the Old Age Security Act, the Department began to assess and collect old age security tax on personal and corporate income.
  • 1966: The Department started collecting Canada Pension Plan (CPP) contributions from employers and self-employed Canadians.
  • 1972: The Department’s role expanded. It began to collect unemployment insurance premiums under a revised Unemployment Insurance Act.
  • 1974: Indexing became part of Canada’s tax system. Personal exemptions increased. Also, each bracket of taxable income started to be changed every year according to an inflation factor based on the consumer price index.
  • 1987: Sixteen years after the federal government passed Bill C-259, the government presented a white paper on tax reform to the Canadian public, which was to be implemented in two phases:
    • redistributing the tax burden, by making a number of changes to the personal, corporate, and sales tax systems
    • replacing the federal sales tax with a broad-based, multistage, sales tax called goods and services tax.
  • 1991: The federal government replaced the federal sales tax with a goods and services tax (GST). The government also introduced a GST credit to help families with low and modest incomes offset the effects of all or part of the new tax.
  • 1996: The federal government announced the formation of a national revenue agency, later named the Canada Customs and Revenue Agency, which would have greater autonomy and flexibility to deliver programs and services.
  • 1997: In Newfoundland and Labrador, Nova Scotia, and New Brunswick, GST was combined with the provincial sales tax to form harmonized sales tax (HST). The related tax credit was the GST/HST credit.
